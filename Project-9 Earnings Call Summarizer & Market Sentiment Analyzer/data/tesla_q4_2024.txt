TESLA Q4 2024 EARNINGS CALL TRANSCRIPT
Date: January 29, 2025

[CEO - Elon Musk]: Good afternoon everyone. Thank you for joining us today. I'm pleased to report that we had a strong quarter with a 12% increase in vehicle deliveries compared to Q4 2023. We delivered a record 495,000 vehicles globally. Our gross margins improved to 18.5%, up from 16.8% last quarter. This was driven by operational efficiencies and lower battery costs.

[CFO - Vaibhav Taneja]: On the financial side, total revenue reached $25.7 billion, representing a 7% year-over-year growth. Operating income remained stable at $2.1 billion. We generated $3.2 billion in free cash flow during the quarter. We are investing heavily in AI-driven logistics and manufacturing automation, which we expect to further improve margins by 2-3% over the next year.

[CEO - Elon Musk]: Looking ahead, we expect vehicle deliveries to grow by 20-25% in 2025. We're seeing strong demand for our new Model 3 refresh and Cybertruck production is ramping up faster than expected. We're also making significant progress on our Full Self-Driving technology, which we believe will be a major revenue driver in the coming years.

[Analyst - John Smith, Goldman Sachs]: Could you clarify how inflation and interest rates are affecting your capital expenditures and consumer demand?

[CFO - Vaibhav Taneja]: Good question. While inflation has moderated, we're still seeing some pressure on component costs. However, our vertical integration strategy has helped mitigate these impacts. We're maintaining our capital expenditure guidance of $9-10 billion for 2025. On consumer demand, we've observed some softening in China, but Europe and North America remain strong.

[Analyst - Sarah Lee, Morgan Stanley]: Can you provide more color on the Full Self-Driving timeline and revenue expectations?

[CEO - Elon Musk]: Full Self-Driving is progressing well. We expect to achieve unsupervised FSD in select markets by the end of 2025. We believe this could be a $10-15 billion revenue opportunity annually once fully deployed. Safety data continues to show that FSD is significantly safer than human drivers.

[Analyst - Michael Chen, JPMorgan]: What about competition from Chinese EV manufacturers?

[CEO - Elon Musk]: We take competition seriously. Our vertical integration, brand loyalty, and technology leadership give us a significant competitive advantage. We're not worried. We're focused on making the best products, and the market continues to prove that.

[CFO - Vaibhav Taneja]: To conclude, we remain very optimistic about 2025. We expect full-year deliveries to reach 2.2 million vehicles, representing approximately 22% growth. We're committed to delivering sustainable value to our shareholders through innovation and operational excellence.

Thank you all for joining today.
